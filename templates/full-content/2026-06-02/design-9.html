Users are dropping off at the 'connect' stage of onboarding, what should I do to improve it

Ah, the dreaded "connect" cliff 😬 — super common, and usually very fixable.

Here's a structured way to attack it, from fastest wins to deeper fixes.

1. First: figure out why they're dropping

Before changing things, get signal on what's actually happening.

Quick checks

  • Where exactly do they drop?
    • Before seeing the connect screen?
    • After clicking "Connect"?
    • After an error / permission prompt?
  • Is drop-off higher on:
    • Mobile vs desktop
    • Specific providers (Google, GitHub, Stripe, etc.)
    • New vs returning users

Low-effort tools

  • Session replays (FullStory, Hotjar, LogRocket)
  • Funnel with step-level error tracking
  • One-question exit poll on the connect screen:

    "What's stopping you from continuing?" (Options + free text)

You'll usually spot fear, confusion, or friction within an hour of watching replays.

2. Reduce perceived risk (this is often the real blocker)

"Connect" sounds scary. Users worry about:

  • Data access
  • Permissions
  • Breaking something
  • Hidden costs

Improvements that often move metrics fast

  • Explicitly say what you do and do not access
    • "We can read X, we cannot modify Y"
  • Explain why the connection is needed in one sentence
    • "We need this to automatically sync your data so you don't have to upload files."
  • Add trust signals:
    • Logos of companies using you
    • "SOC 2 compliant", "Read-only access"
    • Link to security page (even if most won't click)

📌 If users hesitate before clicking connect, this is your main issue.

3. Make the connect step feel smaller

Psychologically, "connect your account" feels like a big commitment.

Ways to shrink it

  • Reframe copy:
    • ❌ "Connect your account"
    • ✅ "Preview your data" / "Test the connection"
  • Show progress:
    • "Step 2 of 4"
    • "~30 seconds"
  • If possible, allow skip for now
    • Even if it limits features, users are more likely to come back than bounce

Counterintuitive truth: letting people skip often increases eventual connection rate.

4. Fix technical friction (boring but lethal)

Common silent killers:

  • OAuth popup blocked
  • Redirect opens new tab without explanation
  • Long loading with no feedback
  • Generic error messages ("Something went wrong")

Best practices

  • Inline loading states ("Connecting to X… this can take ~10 seconds")
  • Specific error messages with recovery:
    • "Your Google account denied access — click here to retry"
  • Retry without restarting onboarding
  • Auto-detect existing sessions (don't ask them to log in again)

If users click connect and nothing obvious happens, you've already lost them.

5. Show value before asking for the connection

If users don't yet believe your product is valuable, they won't hand over access.

Options

  • Demo data / sandbox view
  • Screenshot or mock of their future dashboard
  • One concrete outcome:
    • "After connecting, you'll instantly see X"

Rule of thumb:

Users should want to connect before you ask them to.

6. Personalize the connect step

If you know anything about the user, use it.

Examples:

  • Pre-select the most likely provider
  • "Most teams like yours connect X first"
  • "You'll be able to pull data from your \{company_name} workspace"

Small relevance cues reduce hesitation more than more text.

7. Measure the right success metric

Don't just track "connected or not".

Also track:

  • Clicked "Connect" → success
  • Time spent on connect screen
  • Error rate per provider
  • % who skip and later return

This tells you whether the problem is motivation or execution.

TL;DR (if you want the fast playbook)

  1. Watch 10 session replays
  2. Add 1 sentence explaining why the connection is needed
  3. Make permissions feel safe and reversible
  4. Add progress + time expectation
  5. Improve errors and loading feedback
  6. Let users skip (if possible)
